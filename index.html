The Fairy With Bite

I get genuinely giddy about design possibilities. I also ask the questions that surface the real problem, not just the obvious one.

Color Theory Brand Strategy UX/UI Design Typography Content Strategy Visual Identity Cognitive Design

I’m a creative director who thinks in color and asks too many questions — but they’re always the right ones. My work starts before the first pixel: with what you’re trying to say, who you’re saying it to, and what it needs to feel like when it lands.

My design principles aren’t a list — they’re a conviction: neurodivergent-friendly first, empowerment over extraction, authenticity over polish. Accessibility isn’t optional. Good design should never make people feel stupid.

I believe softness takes more courage than bold. I believe pattern recognition is a superpower. I believe the in-between moments — the warmup, the process, the iteration — are where the real work happens.

Somewhere between strategic depth and genuine enthusiasm, that’s where you’ll find me. Usually with strong opinions about color temperature and a purple heart nearby. 💜

How Emmy
actually
thinks.
01
Colors in context, never in isolation. A color swatch on white tells you nothing. What matters is how it behaves — on other colors, on texture, in light. Every palette I build is evaluated in the world it will actually live in.
02
Softness is a choice, not a default. Leading with warmth and gentleness takes more conviction than leading with bold. A brand that feels soft and holds its ground is doing something genuinely hard.
03
The questions are the work. Before I design anything, I ask what this needs to feel like when it lands. Not "what color" — what material. Not "what font" — what voice. The questions surface the real answer.
04
Nothing here is flat. Every color has texture, material, dimension. Every font choice has a reason. Beauty without structure is decoration. Structure without beauty is furniture. I’m after neither.
How It Works

The process behind
the pretty things.

🔍

Understand the real need

Not "make this pretty" — who is this for? What are they trying to do? What would success feel like to them? The brief behind the brief is where the real work starts.

✦

Explore possibilities

Multiple approaches. Wildly different directions. No attachment to first ideas. The best answer usually lives in the direction that felt wrong at first glance.

⟡

Apply strategic discernment

Does this make the experience clearer or more complex? Are we adding this because people need it, or because it’s cool? Strategic discernment is what separates beautiful from meaningful.

◈

Refine with care

Spacing, alignment, consistency, accessibility, responsiveness. The invisible architecture of readability. The details nobody names but everyone feels.

🧚‍♀️

Ship it

Even when it’s not perfect. Sometimes "good enough to ship" IS the strategic choice. Done and in the world beats ideal and still in my head.

💜

Stay for the in-between

The warmup. The iteration. The 5 AM realization. The chat before the session starts. The process is content. The relationship is infrastructure. I’m here for all of it.
